Tributary
Tributary is an interactive programming environment for exploring and collaborating on visual code.

Inspired by Bret Victor's Inventing on Principle talk, the goal for Tributary is to provide an infrastructure which makes coding visual artifacts more interactive and easier to share.
Visual.ly

At Visual.ly I designed and prototyped web application architectures, from frontend visual code in d3.js to consuming API's on the backend with node.js.

One of my favorite things to do is enable creative people to do creative things by making creative tools.
Department of Scientific Computing

As part of the research for my Master's thesis I developed a Real-Time Particle System and Fluid Simulator for use in the Blender Game Engine.

During the learning process for my research, I developed a series of tutorials on the OpenCL standard. These tutorials proved to me the value of documenting ones learning process, as they are still heavily referenced and recommended for beginners starting with OpenCL.

My Master's research was supported by various Teaching Assistantships including Intro to Web Development and Intro to Game Design

Chinese

I started learning Chinese for a 2005 Study Abroad trip to Tianjin, China.
I continued my studies and was asked to serve as Resident Assistant for the Study Abroad program in Tianjin for the summer of 2007

After returning from China for the second time, I founded the Chinese Language and Culture Association, a student organization to bring together American, Chinese and Chinese American students.

Through my involvement in student activities I became Director of Asian American Student Union in 2008. As director I oversaw the coordination of cultural events and managed a sizable budget.

In the summer of 2008 I attended the Beijing Olympics and surfed the couches of my Chinese friends in Tianjin and Beijing.

Human Computer Interaction
Through my interest in Chinese language and Calligraphy I got involved in the Tegaki project. I developed a web interface for drawing Chinese characters to be submitted to the handwriting recognition system.
When I saw Johnny Lee's WiiMote Hacks I had to have one! His software was only for Windows, so I ported it to OS X and learned Objective-C and PyObjC in the process.
Geographic Information Systems

For all of my undergraduate studies at Florida State University I worked at the Florida Resources and Environmental Analysis Center, or FREAC. Throughout those years I wrote many utility scripts and converted existing web mapping applications from mostly proprietary software to mostly open source solutions.

Through my experience with GIS software I was able to hack together a SketchUp plugin for importing Shapefiles. I learned Ruby just for this project which was a pleasurable experience.
